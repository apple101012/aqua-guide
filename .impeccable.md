## Design Context

### Users
Mix of local residents in water-stressed areas, travelers/visitors, students, renters, and aid workers. They use the app both proactively (planning ahead, checking conditions before travel) and in real-time ("is this water safe right now?"). Context ranges from calm research on a laptop to urgent checks on a phone.

### Brand Personality
Trusted, clear, warm. Should feel like a civic-grade product -- authoritative enough to trust with safety decisions, but warm and approachable enough that anyone can use it without feeling intimidated. Empowering: "you know what to do."

### Aesthetic Direction
Modern utility app -- think well-designed weather or maps apps (Dark Sky, Apple Weather, Google Maps). NOT a government/NGO website. NOT a startup landing page. Light theme overall with dark map section. Keep the teal/navy water palette but elevate execution to feel premium: better typography, refined spacing, intentional hierarchy. The interface should make judges ask "how was this made?" not "which template is this?"

### Design Principles
1. **Instant clarity** -- the first 10 seconds must be visceral and understandable
2. **Trust through craft** -- source citations, update timestamps, and polished UI build credibility
3. **Action over information** -- every screen should answer "what do I do next?"
4. **Accessible by default** -- not a toggle, but baked into the core experience
5. **Product, not prototype** -- it should feel finished, like something people already use
